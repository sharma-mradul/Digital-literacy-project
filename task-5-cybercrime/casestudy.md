# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## Overview

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India. It involves scammers tricking users into transferring money or sharing sensitive banking information through fake payment requests, links, or calls.

## How the Crime Happens

First, the scammer contacts the victim through a phone call, WhatsApp message, or fake advertisement. They pretend to be a bank officer, online seller, or customer care executive. Next, they send a fake UPI payment request or link and ask the victim to approve it to receive money. In some cases, they ask the victim to install a screen-sharing app or share OTP and UPI PIN. Once the victim enters the PIN or approves the request, money is instantly transferred to the scammer’s account. The fraud usually happens within a few minutes.

## Targeted People

Students, elderly people, online shoppers, and people unfamiliar with digital payments are usually targeted. New UPI users are especially vulnerable because they may not understand how payment requests work.

## Consequences

Victims lose their money and often cannot recover it. It also causes stress, financial loss, and loss of trust in digital payment systems. In some cases, bank accounts may get blocked during investigation.

## Prevention

People should never share OTP or UPI PIN, should verify payment requests carefully, and should only use official banking apps. Awareness and digital literacy can prevent such cybercrimes.
